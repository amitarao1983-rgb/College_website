Departments

Our academic departments provide subject-focused training, laboratory exposure, and research guidance aligned with PCI norms and healthcare needs.

Department of Pharmacology

The Department of Pharmacology is an essential part of the institution, focusing on the study of how drugs interact with the human body and their role in the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of diseases. The department provides a strong combination of theoretical knowledge and practical training, helping students understand drug actions, dosage, side effects, toxicity, and therapeutic uses. Teaching is delivered through structured classroom sessions along with hands-on laboratory experiments, ensuring effective learning and real-time understanding. The department is supported by well-equipped laboratories with instruments such as actophotometer and other standard pharmacology equipment for practical training. In addition, modern learning methods like computer-based simulations and virtual lab software are used to promote ethical and advanced education. Experienced and qualified faculty members guide students in academics, practical sessions, and research activities, helping them build strong knowledge and skills. Students are encouraged to participate in seminars, workshops, guest lectures, and research projects, which improve their analytical thinking and professional development. The department also emphasizes safe laboratory practices, proper handling of equipment, and professional ethics. Overall, it aims to develop competent, responsible, and skilled pharmacy professionals who can contribute effectively to healthcare, research, and the pharmaceutical industry.

Department of Pharmaceutical Chemistry

The Department of Pharmaceutical Chemistry is a vital part of our institution, dedicated to excellence in education, research, and innovation in the field of drug discovery and development. The department aims to produce competent, ethical, and skilled pharmacy professionals who can meet the growing demands of the healthcare and pharmaceutical industries, while providing a strong academic foundation in key areas such as medicinal chemistry, pharmaceutical analysis, organic chemistry, biochemistry, and drug design. Through a well-structured curriculum, students gain both theoretical knowledge and practical skills essential for drug development, quality control, and regulatory understanding. The department is supported by experienced and highly qualified faculty members who guide students in academics and research activities, supported by well-equipped laboratories with modern instruments such as UV-Visible Spectrophotometer, HPLC system, pH meter, and other analytical tools that ensure excellent practical exposure. The laboratories are designed to support synthesis, analysis, and research work with proper safety measures and standard protocols. Students are encouraged to actively participate in research projects, seminars, workshops, and guest lectures, helping them stay updated with the latest advancements in pharmaceutical sciences, while promoting professional ethics, social responsibility, and continuous learning. Overall, the department strives to create knowledgeable and skilled graduates who contribute effectively to healthcare and society.

Department of Pharmaceutics

The Department of Pharmaceutics focuses on the design, formulation, and development of pharmaceutical dosage forms such as tablets, capsules, syrups, and other drug delivery systems. The department provides a strong foundation in drug formulation, manufacturing processes, and quality evaluation, helping students understand how medicines are prepared for safe and effective use. Teaching is delivered through structured theory classes along with hands-on laboratory training, enabling students to gain practical experience in formulation techniques and equipment handling. The department is supported by well-equipped laboratories with instruments such as tablet compression machine, dissolution apparatus, and other pharmaceutics equipment, which provide real-time exposure to pharmaceutical manufacturing processes. Experienced and qualified faculty members guide students in academic learning, practical skills, and research activities, helping them build strong technical knowledge. Students are encouraged to participate in seminars, workshops, and formulation-based projects to enhance their understanding of modern drug delivery systems. The department also emphasizes good manufacturing practices (GMP), quality assurance, and professional ethics. Overall, it aims to develop competent, skilled, and industry-ready pharmacy professionals who can contribute effectively to the pharmaceutical industry and healthcare sector.

Department of Pharmacognosy

The Department of Pharmacognosy focuses on the study of medicinal plants and natural sources of drugs, helping students understand the identification, collection, and evaluation of crude drugs used in healthcare. The department provides a strong foundation in herbal medicine, phytochemistry, and natural product research, enabling students to learn how plant-based medicines are developed and used safely. Teaching is delivered through structured theory classes along with hands-on laboratory training, where students gain practical experience in microscopy, extraction, and identification of plant materials. The department is supported by well-equipped laboratories with facilities for studying crude drugs, herbal formulations, and plant analysis, providing real-time exposure to natural product research. Experienced and qualified faculty members guide students in academic learning, practical work, and research activities, helping them build strong knowledge in herbal sciences. Students are encouraged to participate in seminars, workshops, and research projects related to medicinal plants and traditional systems of medicine. The department also emphasizes quality control of herbal drugs, sustainable use of natural resources, and professional ethics. Overall, it aims to develop knowledgeable and skilled pharmacy professionals who can contribute effectively to herbal medicine, research, and the pharmaceutical industry.

Department of Pharmaceutical Analysis

The Department of Pharmaceutical Analysis focuses on the study of identification, testing, and quality control of drugs to ensure their safety, purity, and effectiveness. The department provides a strong foundation in analytical techniques and instrumental methods, helping students understand drug analysis, validation, and regulatory standards. Teaching is delivered through structured theory classes along with hands-on laboratory training, enabling students to gain practical experience in modern analytical methods. The department is supported by well-equipped laboratories with instruments such as UV-Visible spectrophotometer, pH meter, and other analytical equipment, which help in accurate testing and evaluation of pharmaceutical substances. Experienced and qualified faculty members guide students in academic learning, practical work, and research activities, enhancing their technical and scientific skills. Students are encouraged to participate in seminars, workshops, and research projects to stay updated with recent advancements in pharmaceutical analysis. The department also emphasizes good laboratory practices (GLP), data accuracy, and professional ethics. Overall, it aims to develop competent and skilled pharmacy professionals who can contribute effectively to quality control, research, and the pharmaceutical industry.
